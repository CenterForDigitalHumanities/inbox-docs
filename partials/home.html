Interesting Scholarly Content

The Rerum Inbox is a place for anyone to post and read announcements about any digital resource published on the Internet. A strong supporter of the IIIF standard, it is the sc:Manifest objects for which this has been created. The millions of images made available by libraries, museums, universities, and others throughout the world are now enriched by the linked knowledge of a planet full of scholars and researchers. Only the most relevant and intentional notes are intended for this announcement inbox, so the information is more immediately useful than aggregations that simply crawl the graph of Linked Data looking for relationships without discrimination.

Community-Driven Specifications

Fulfilling the promise of Linked Data requires both the robotic and shared language of RDF and the human conventions within each field that help discern relevance. The standard of Linked Data Notifications, when applied to the distributed resources used in Digital Humanities research, makes possible and then highlights the connections between the exhibit of the digital surrogates and the community of scholarly assertions and descriptions that exist elsewhere on the graph.

RERUM

reconditorium eximium rerum universalium mutabiliumque

The Rerum Inbox is a service of the Walter J. Ong, S.J. Center for Digital Humanities under the umbrella of rerum.io. As we develop more tools and services associated with RERUM, it remains our goal to advance standards through automatic compliance, to reduce the obstacles preventing large institutions from sharing and small institutions from contributing, and to model increasingly effective and durable ways for scholars and researchers to regenerate and synthesize knowledge through conversation around existing resources.

OngCDH works in the open in several places, but is most frequently seen on Github. We thrive on collaborations, big and small, and would love to hear about your project or whim, even if you have no funding available.
